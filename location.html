Vander Ende-Onderdonk House

A cute vintage farm house in the middle of the hipster industrial wasteland of Bushwick, Brooklyn, will be the setting of our wedding.

The Vander Ende-Onderdonk House is the oldest Dutch colonial stone house in New York City. Peter Stuyvesant granted the land it sits on in the mid-seventeenth century, and by 1660, Hendrick Barents Smidt occupied a small house on the site. In 1709, Paulus Vander Ende of Flatbush purchased the farm and began construction of the current house.

The house was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1977. It was designated a landmark by the New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission in 1995.

How to Get There

You can easily get to the Onderdonk House from anywhere within Manhattan or Brooklyn using public transportation, taxi, or car.

Driving / Parking:
If you are driving, there will be street parking on the side of the house, on Onderdonk Avenue.

Taxi / Uber / Lyft:
A taxi from Manhattan will take around 30 minutes and cost approximately $30 - $40. By law they can only sit four people at a time. In Brooklyn, it will take you 15 minutes, and cost $20 - $25.

If you have a smart phone, we recommend using Uber or Lyft as it will be easier to hail rides at the end of the night, and the price is similar to that of a taxi.

Public Transportation / Metro:
Take the L train to Jefferson Street in Brooklyn. It will be a short 10 minute walk to the Onderdonk House from the train station.

We recommend taking a stroll down Troutman Street on your way there to see all the beautiful street art.

Where to Stay

Since it is relatively easy to get to the Onderdonk House from anywhere within NYC, you should stay wherever best fits your budget.

If you plan on sightseeing, staying in Manhattan would be your best bet.
